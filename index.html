IPS Infrastructure · Three Scenarios
Provenance is not permission.
C2PA tells you where content came from. DDT tells you whether use of the identity depicted in that content is currently authorized — live, revocable, machine-verifiable. These are orthogonal claims. Both are required.
The OCSP analogy: A browser doesn't ask a human to verify a TLS certificate — it queries an OCSP responder automatically at the moment of the handshake. IPS is the same ambient, automatic consent check. The CRO is the certificate. The IPS endpoint is the responder. The ddt.cro_binding in the C2PA manifest is the pointer that ties the asset to the consent infrastructure for its entire downstream life. Every query generates a signed audit packet. The record exists whether the requester liked the answer or not.
