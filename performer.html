What happens when someone tries to strip your consent record
They can erase the file.
They can't erase you.
Your consent isn't just in the file metadata. It's embedded in three separate layers. Even if someone removes every visible trace, your CRO can still be recovered.
01
✗
They strip the file metadata
The C2PA manifest is removed. Your name and CRO are gone from the file header.
Waiting
This is the most common attack. Metadata is easy to strip — a simple conversion or re-export can remove it. This is why Layer 1 is never the only layer.
02
◎
The watermark survives
Your CRO pointer is embedded invisibly in the pixels. It survives compression, cropping, and format changes.
Waiting
The TrustMark or Digimarc watermark is injected at the pixel level — not the metadata level. Resizing, recompressing, screenshotting: it still survives. Your CRO ID can be recovered from the image itself.
03
◈
The neural fingerprint is the last line
Even if the watermark is scrubbed, your content can still be matched to your CRO record.
Waiting
A neural content hash — derived from the content itself, not injected — resolves to your CRO record even if both other layers have been removed. There is no clean scrub.
04
⟳
IPS is queried — your consent record answers
All three paths lead to the same endpoint. Your terms stand.
Waiting
Whether it's Layer 1, 2, or 3 that resolves the CRO — the IPS query is the same. Your permission state is returned. The audit packet is generated. The system works regardless of the attack.
